What is Science?

Science is the systematic study of the structure and behavior of the physical and natural world through observation, experimentation, and the testing of theories against evidence. It represents humanity's best effort to understand the universe and our place within it.

Through rigorous methodologies and empirical verification, science provides us with a reliable framework for understanding the natural world. It's a dynamic process of discovery that continuously refines our understanding of reality through careful observation, hypothesis testing, and peer review.

The scientific method serves as the foundation for all scientific inquiry, providing a systematic approach to investigating phenomena, acquiring new knowledge, and correcting and integrating existing knowledge. It's a self-correcting process that leads to increasingly accurate descriptions of the natural world.

Science is not just a collection of facts, but a way of thinking - a method for understanding the universe that surrounds us, from the smallest quantum particles to the vast expanses of space.

"Science is not only compatible with spirituality; it is a profound source of spirituality. When we recognize our place in an immensity of light-years and in the passage of ages, when we grasp the intricacy, beauty, and subtlety of life, then that soaring feeling, that sense of elation and humility combined, is surely spiritual."
Carl Sagan (American astronomer and science communicator)
